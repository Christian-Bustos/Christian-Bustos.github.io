Optimization Methods for Clustering

We considered the Maximum Clique Problem (MCP) in graph theory using the Frank-Wolfe algorithm and its variants. We implemented and evaluated classic Frank-Wolfe, Away-Step Frank-Wolfe, and Pairwise Frank-Wolfe algorithms with different regularization terms. Through numerical experiments on DIMACS benchmark datasets, we assessed the algorithms' performance, focusing on computational efficiency and solution quality.

Sentiment Analysis of Customer Reviews: A Comparative Analysis

This Analysis involves a comparative study of various sentiment analysis techniques on the Amazon US Customer Reviews Dataset. The models used include VADER, BERT, RoBERTa, and a multi-label logistic regression model. We focused on classifying sentiments into positive, negative, and neutral categories. Our findings highlight the superior performance of transformer-based models like BERT and RoBERTa over traditional methods, demonstrating their enhanced contextual understanding and accuracy in sentiment classification.

Bankruptcy Classification and Stastisical Analysis

The focus is on predicting the likelihood of company bankruptcy using statistical models. Utilizing the Compustat dataset, which includes 5436 observations of financial variables, we implemented various models including logistic regression, balanced logistic models, and shrinkage methods like Ridge and Lasso regression. Our analysis aims to identify key financial indicators that contribute to the risk of bankruptcy, offering insights into the importance of factors such as working capital, retained earnings, and market capitalization in assessing a company's financial health.

Comparing Humans, GPT-4, and Claude 2 On Abstraction and Reasoning Tasks

Investigation of the abstract reasoning capabilities of AI models, specifically GPT-4 and Claude 2, using the ConceptARC dataset. The study compares the performance of these models with human solvers on analogy puzzles designed to assess abstract reasoning. Results show that humans consistently outperform both AI models, with Claude 2 lagging behind GPT-4.

Simulation of random variables and Monte Carlo method: theoretical aspects and applications

This thesis examines the Monte Carlo method and its use in financial decision-making. It covers random variable generation, detailing True and Pseudo Random Number Generators, and introduces the Linear Congruential Generator. The thesis demonstrates Monte Carlo applications, including stock market predictions, combining theoretical insights with R code implementations to showcase its effectiveness in finance.

Deep Learning Models for COVID-19, Pneumonia, and Normal Chest X-ray Classification

This study evaluates the performance of ResNet, DenseNet, and a custom CNN in classifying chest X-ray images into COVID-19, pneumonia, and normal categories. Using the COVID-19 Radiography Database with 4,575 images, the models were trained with preprocessing techniques like resizing and padding, and data augmentation. The models are build from scratch by using Keras and TensorFlow.
